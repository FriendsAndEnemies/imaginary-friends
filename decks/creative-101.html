01Creative 101 · The problem

Attention is divided

Everyone’s fighting for a shrinking sliver of it — audiences and brands in the same crowded field, everything shouting at once.
02The problem

You can’t just make noise — you need signal

Noise is broadcasting into the crowd and hoping something sticks — one more indistinguishable circle in a field that’s already full. Nobody notices, and it costs nothing to ignore.
Signal is built to connect with a specific person, not shaped to maximize reach. It isn’t louder — it’s more precise. Fewer people, on purpose. That’s exactly why it gets through.
03The definition

Signal needs a source and a recipient

To connect, signal needs both ends of the line. Skip either one and there’s nothing to connect.
Source
Who You Are
The brand. What you stand for, and what makes you worth paying attention to.
Recipient
Who You’re For
The audience. The specific people the signal is built to reach.
04The definition

Why creative matters — and it’s not just taste

Insight from real life→ Creative — the bridge→ The audience acts
It starts with an insight from the audience’s real life — something true and specific about how they think or behave, not a demographic stat.
Creative is the bridge: it ties that insight to the brand, and to a message, moment, or outcome the brand can own — to get the audience to act.
So it can’t be judged on taste alone. The test is whether it’s rooted in a real insight and built to move someone — a business question, not an aesthetic one.

09Toolkit · Frameworks

The loyalty funnel

We work up and down the funnel — creating impact at every stage, not just the top.
The goal: find new audiences and make them aware of the brand.
Get them to engage, then transact — convert.
And ultimately, become loyal — which feeds the next cycle.
10Toolkit · Frameworks

The marketing flywheel

Marketing is a flywheel, not a one-off.
Build a campaign, launch it, and measure what happens.
Learn from those measurements, refine, and apply it to the next execution.
Every turn feeds the next — the loop builds momentum over time.
